Light Node

Room space in the libraries at Carnegie Mellon has always been a problem, with students often times walking around for hours just trying to find an empty room. The idea behind this project was to create a visually appealing way to determine the occupancy of a room inside. Motion, distance, and sound sensors determined student's movements and displayed current occupancy through a combination of LED lights.This would let students simply look at this indicator to determine the room’s availability.

  • Video: Light Node
  • Date: November 2015
  • Service: Arduino, Pixie LEDs, C, & 3D Printing

Research in Short Term Memory Recall

As part of a team, I studied the relationship between kerning and serifs and short term memory. Discovering that ideally sans-serif fonts match well with low kerning while serif fonts match well with high kerning.

  • Date: Spring 2015
  • Skills: Psych.js, Amazon Mechanical Turk
